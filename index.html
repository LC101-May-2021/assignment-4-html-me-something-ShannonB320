Lifetime Builder

"Enthusiast" and "collector" are words that come close, but what can truly describe being an Adult Fan of Lego?

As a younger kid, most of my exposure to Lego building bricks came through my brother Tony. He was five years older and could be trusted with smaller choke-hazard type toys. Nonetheless, his fervor for building meant Tony and I spent many days together building Lego cities and scenes. Now, even as I’ve gotten older, I haven’t lost my love I have for building. Thus, I have joined the community of “Adult Fans of Lego” (or AFOL).

What is there besides blocks?

The Lego community is strong, especially online. The Lego website itself has A COMMUNITY BOARD where Lego fans can collaborate. There are also dedicated websites (like BRICKLINK, for instance) for fans of Lego to buy/sell/trade parts and share their own original designs. The fun doesn’t stay virtual, though! Recently Fox’s LEGO MASTERS premiered, where teams compete to see if their mega-sized builds can hold up to various tests. Worldwide there are 27 Lego Discovery Centers where visitors can see life-size Lego sculptures and participate in themed activities for all ages. Every year there are dozens of fan expos and conventions well as the official annual LEGO(R) CON.

So you're just following instructions?

One of the most interesting aspects of the AFOL community is “alternate builds.” These are creative constructions that take a branded Lego set and use the pieces to make something entirely new. Other artists challenge themselves to create completely original Lego builds, including assembling entire cities!
